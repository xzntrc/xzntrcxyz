How is it possible to change your gender?

Simply put, it's impossible, now to come to such a conclusion; we must first take a look at basic science; the same sort of science that atheists use to defend their points (I promise to not get religious on this one). To explain briefly, the human body is comprised of many microscopic cells (typically the diameter of these cells are less than a tenth of a human hair), these cells make up everything on our body. Take a look at your "pinky-toe"; while being the smallest toe, it has around 2 - 3 billion cells inside it. The significance of these cells can be examined when you understand what is inside of each of them; Chromosomes, which are tightly packaged "thread-like" structures that form our DNA (think of it as our "genetic code" if you will).

Each chromosome is the genetic information, or DNA, that is responsible for coding for certain traits. For the human person, there are 23 pairs of chromosomes or 46 total chromosomes. Each of the pairs comes from the parent's DNA, one from the mother and one from the father. Looking at each of the 23 pairs of chromosomes, they code for certain body parts, the color of hair and skin, as well as height of the individual. The last pair of chromosomes are known as the sex chromosomes; every man has the same sex chromosomes (X and a Y), and every woman has the same (X and an X). It is crucial that the body knows who it is. For example, whenever your hair grows; it's always the same color, because the cells of your body make it that way; and is scientifically impossible to change. This applies to sex as well.
